I am a fourth year undergraduate at the University of Califorrnia, Berkeley. I am pursuing a Bachelors of Science in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science.

I am a researcher in the Salahuddin Group working in the field of spintronics. My interests include solid-state devices, integrated circuits, and embedded systems. I hope to use cutting-edge solid state physics and recent spintronics research to create new, more efficient, better scalable electronic devices using techniques such as ferromagnetic resonance, multiferroics, and spin torque.

I also have computer science experience from working on research for Lawrence Berkeley National Lab. During my time at Berkeley I have been involved with the Cal Solar Car team, building a competitive solar-powered race car, and the ASUC (Associated Students of the University of California) Office of the CTO, leading a project to create a system of campus-wide density scanners.
