I am a first-year Master's student at the University of Califorrnia, Berkeley, pursuing a degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS). I recently graduated from Berkeley Class of 2017 with a Bachelor's of Science in EECS.

I work as a researcher in the Salahuddin Group, in the Laboratory for Emerging and Exploratory Devices (LEED), working in the field of spintronics. My interests include solid-state devices, integrated circuits, and embedded systems. Our lab hopes to use cutting-edge solid state physics and recent spintronics research to create new, more efficient, better scalable electronic devices using techniques such as ferromagnetic resonance, multiferroics, and spin torque.

I also have computer science experience from working on research for Lawrence Berkeley National Lab. During my time at Berkeley I have been involved with the Cal Solar Car team, building a competitive solar-powered race car, and the ASUC Office of the CTO, leading a project to create a system of campus-wide density scanners.
